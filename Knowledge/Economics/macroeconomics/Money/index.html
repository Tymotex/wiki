    # Money Measures

    In modern economies, money takes the form of physical currency (notes/coins) and private bank deposits.

    # Money Supply

    Money supply is the amount of money circulating in the economy. It can be a measure of currency, M1, or other standard measures of money.

    # Money Demand

    Money demand is basically how much money we, in aggregate, would like to hold in our bank accounts (as opposed to lower liquidity assets): $$\colorbox{#FFFFBF}{$M^D=P\times L(Y, i)$},$$ where $P$ is the aggregate price level and $L$ is the liquidity preference function, which is a function of real GDP and interest rate. A decrease in the opportunity cost of holding money will shift the money demand curve up.
